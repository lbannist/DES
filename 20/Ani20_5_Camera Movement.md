# Animation 20 – Camera Movement

## Animation Production Techniques: Cinematic Storytelling

**Time:** 1 Week (4–5 Class Periods)

**Due Date:** Following Monday before class

---

# Learning Objectives

By the end of this assignment students will:

* Create purposeful camera movement
* Improve visual storytelling
* Use framing and composition effectively
* Create dynamic scenes without complex character animation
* Understand how camera movement affects audience attention

---

# Production Question

## How can a static drawing become an engaging animation?

Professional productions often create movement through the camera rather than through characters.

A well-designed camera move can create excitement while dramatically reducing production time.

---

# Part 1 – Camera Analysis (Day 1)

Study examples of:

* Panning shots
* Zooms
* Tracking shots
* Reveals
* Push-ins

### Observation Questions

1. What is the camera trying to show?
2. What information is revealed?
3. How does movement affect mood?
4. When does the camera speed up or slow down?

---

# Part 2 – Scene Planning (Day 1)

Choose a scene:

* Abandoned building
* Fantasy landscape
* City street
* Space station
* Original approved environment

Sketch:

* Starting camera position
* Ending camera position
* Key points of interest

---

# Part 3 – Animation Production (Days 2–4)

### Requirements

* 10–15 second animation
* Minimum 2 camera movements
* Background larger than viewport
* Camera motion must feel intentional
* Minimal character animation allowed

---

# Part 4 – Reflection (Day 5)

1. What did the camera reveal?
2. How did camera movement improve storytelling?
3. What animation work was avoided through camera motion?
4. What would you improve next time?
