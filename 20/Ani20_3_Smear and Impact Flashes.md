---

# Production Question

## How can one frame make an action feel ten times faster?

Professional animators rarely add more drawings to make something feel fast.

Instead they use:

* Smear frames
* Impact flashes
* Exaggerated poses

These techniques increase energy while reducing workload.

---

# Part 1 – Action Analysis (Day 1)

Study examples of:

* Punches
* Kicks
* Sword swings
* Dashes
* Explosions

### Observation Questions

1. Which frame feels fastest?
2. Where is motion blurred or distorted?
3. When does the impact occur?
4. What makes the hit feel powerful?

---

# Part 2 – Planning (Day 1)

Choose an action sequence:

* Punch
* Kick
* Sword slash
* Energy blast
* Dash attack
* Original approved action

### Plan

Create sketches for:

* Start Pose
* Smear Frame
* Impact Frame
* Recovery Pose

---

# Part 3 – Animation Production (Days 2–4)

### Document Settings

* 1920 × 1080
* 12 FPS

### Requirements

* 3–5 second animation
* Minimum 2 smear frames
* At least 1 impact flash
* Clear action sequence
* Before, during, and after poses

Optional:

* Camera shake
* Glow effect
* Dust effects

---

# Part 4 – Efficiency Reflection (Day 5)

Answer:

1. How many smear frames were used?
2. How much animation was replaced by smears?
3. What made the action feel powerful?
4. Which frame is most important?
5. How could you make the animation faster without adding frames?
