# Grade 11 Animation – Production Workflow Course Plan (Krita)

## Course Overview

This Grade 11 Animation course focuses on **production efficiency, cinematic techniques, and professional workflow habits** using Krita.

Students are trained to:
- Animate faster without sacrificing quality
- Reuse assets and cycles effectively
- Combine multiple animation techniques in single scenes
- Work like a production studio rather than individual frame-by-frame artists

---

## Course Structure

- **Weeks 1–10:** Core Assignments (1 per week)
- **Weeks 11–12:** Project 1 (2 weeks)
- **Weeks 13–14:** Project 2 (2 weeks)
- **Weeks 15–16:** Buffer / Final polish / presentation / evaluation

> Note: CAD module occupies first 2 weeks prior to this sequence

---

# WEEKLY ASSIGNMENTS (Weeks 1–10)

## Week 1 – Walk Cycle Efficiency (FOUNDATION)

**Due:** Monday End of Week 1

### Task
Create a reusable walk cycle and apply it in motion.

### Requirements
- Complete looping walk cycle
- Character moves across screen using cycle reuse
- Minimum 6–10 seconds total animation
- At least one reused animation loop

### Focus Skills
- Looping animation
- Timing consistency
- Asset reuse

### Why It Matters
Walk cycles are the backbone of animation pipelines and introduce production efficiency early.

---

## Week 2 – Anticipation + Overshoot (COMBINED)

**Due:** Monday End of Week 2

### Task
Animate a single action that includes both anticipation and overshoot.

### Requirements
- 4–6 second animation
- Clear anticipation pose
- Main action
- Overshoot and settle
- At least 1 secondary motion element

### Focus Skills
- Weight and physics illusion
- Motion staging
- Timing arcs

### Why It Matters
This teaches that motion quality comes from preparation and recovery—not just action.

---

## Week 3 – Smear Frames + Impact Flash (COMBINED)

**Due:** Monday End of Week 3

### Task
Create a fast action scene using smear frames and impact effects.

### Requirements
- 3–5 second animation
- Minimum 2 smear frames
- At least 1 impact flash frame
- Clear readability of motion

### Focus Skills
- Speed illusion
- Exaggeration
- Frame efficiency

### Why It Matters
Students learn to reduce workload while increasing perceived speed and impact.

---

## Week 4 – Environmental Effects + Lighting/Glow (COMBINED)

**Due:** Monday End of Week 4

### Task
Create an animated atmospheric scene with environment and lighting effects.

### Requirements
- 8–12 second animation
- At least 3 environmental effects
- At least 1 glow/light animation
- At least 1 secondary motion element
- Layered depth (foreground/mid/background)

### Focus Skills
- Atmosphere creation
- Lighting design
- Looping effects

### Why It Matters
Environment can carry animation quality without heavy character animation.

---

## Week 5 – Camera Movement

**Due:** Monday End of Week 5

### Task
Create a cinematic scene driven primarily by camera movement.

### Requirements
- 10–15 second animation
- At least 2 camera movements
- Large background scene
- Minimal character animation

### Focus Skills
- Cinematography
- Composition
- Scene framing

### Why It Matters
Camera movement creates cinematic quality without increasing drawing workload.

---

## Week 6 – Parallax Scene Movement

**Due:** Monday End of Week 6

### Task
Build a multi-layered parallax environment.

### Requirements
- Minimum 4 layers of depth
- Continuous camera movement
- 10–15 second animation
- At least 1 animated environmental element

### Focus Skills
- Depth creation
- Layer management
- Scene construction

### Why It Matters
Parallax simulates complex worlds using simple layered assets.

---

## Week 7 – Tweening & Transform Animation

**Due:** Monday End of Week 7

### Task
Create animation primarily using transforms instead of frame-by-frame drawing.

### Requirements
- 5–10 second animation
- Minimum 3 transformed objects
- Use position, rotation, scale, opacity
- Maximum 5 drawn frames

### Focus Skills
- Keyframing
- Motion design
- Workflow efficiency

### Why It Matters
This mirrors real-world motion graphics workflows.

---

## Week 8 – Secondary Motion Integration Challenge

**Due:** Monday End of Week 8

### Task
Animate a character or object where secondary motion is emphasized across movement.

### Requirements
- 5–10 second animation
- At least 2 secondary motion elements (hair, cloth, accessories, etc.)
- Must be integrated into previous techniques learned

### Focus Skills
- Motion layering
- Follow-through
- Realism enhancement

### Why It Matters
Adds realism without requiring additional major animation workload.

---

## Week 9 – Hybrid Animation Challenge

**Due:** Monday End of Week 9

### Task
Combine multiple techniques into one short animation.

### Requirements
Must include:
- Camera movement
- Environmental effect
- Either smear OR impact flash
- Either anticipation OR overshoot
- Minimum 8 seconds

### Focus Skills
- Technique integration
- Workflow planning
- Scene composition

### Why It Matters
Prepares students for full production expectations.

---

## Week 10 – Pre-Project Production Sprint

**Due:** Monday End of Week 10

### Task
Create a short planning + animation test sequence for final project.

### Requirements
- 5–8 second animation test
- Asset reuse required
- Evidence of planning (shot breakdown or layout sketch)
- At least 2 learned techniques included

### Focus Skills
- Pre-production workflow
- Planning efficiency
- Asset organization

### Why It Matters
Students transition from exercises into production thinking.

---

# PROJECT 1 (Weeks 11–12)

## Animated Sequence Project

### Task
Create a 20–30 second cinematic animation sequence.

### Requirements
Must include:
- Walk cycle reuse
- Anticipation + overshoot
- Smear frame OR impact flash
- Environmental effect
- Lighting/glow effect
- Camera movement
- Parallax background

### Deliverables
- Final animation
- Asset folder
- Brief reflection (efficiency choices)

### Focus
Production integration of all core techniques

---

# PROJECT 2 (Weeks 13–14)

## Cinematic Trailer Project

### Task
Create a 30–45 second animated trailer or cinematic intro.

### Requirements
Must include:
- Full integration of all techniques learned
- Multiple scenes or shots
- Strong camera direction
- Environmental storytelling
- Efficient asset reuse

### Deliverables
- Final animation
- Production breakdown (simple timeline or shot list)
- Reflection on workflow efficiency

### Focus
Professional-level workflow simulation

---

# WEEKS 15–16

## Finalization & Presentation

- Final polish submissions
- Peer review screenings
- Teacher evaluation
- Portfolio export preparation
- Optional revisions for improvement

---

# Course Philosophy

This course is designed to teach:

> “Better animation does not come from more drawings—it comes from better decisions.”

Students are evaluated on:
- Efficiency
- Clarity of motion
- Technique integration
- Workflow management
