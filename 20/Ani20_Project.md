# DES 2020 – Animated Storytelling Challenge

## Final Production Project

**Time:** 2 Weeks (10 Class Periods)

**Due Date:** End of Week 2

**Minimum Length:** 30 Seconds

---

# Project Overview

This project simulates a professional animation production challenge similar to those found in Skills Alberta competitions and industry animation studios.

Students will create a complete animated sequence based on a provided theme while incorporating a required object selected at random.

The goal is to demonstrate:

* Storytelling
* Animation principles
* Production planning
* Time management
* Artistic development
* Professional workflow habits

Unlike previous assignments, students will be responsible for managing an entire production pipeline from concept development through final export.

---

# Essential Question

## How do professional animators turn an idea into a finished production on a deadline?

Successful animation projects are not created by drawing continuously.

They are created through planning, organization, and efficient production decisions.

Students will be evaluated on both the quality of their animation and their ability to follow a structured production workflow.

---

# Project Requirements

Students must create:

* A complete animated story
* Minimum 30 seconds in length
* Beginning, middle, and end
* One clear conflict, challenge, discovery, or goal
* One required object selected at random
* Multiple animation techniques learned throughout the course

---

A theme will be provided by the instructor.

# Required Object Challenge

Each student will randomly receive one object from the table below.

The object must play a meaningful role within the story.

The object may be:

* A major plot element
* A clue
* A tool
* A source of conflict
* A reward
* A mystery

The object cannot simply appear in the background.

| Key          | Lantern          | Compass    | Book        |
| ------------ | ---------------- | ---------- | ----------- |
| Helmet       | Crystal          | Map        | Coin        |
| Pocket Watch | Mask             | Camera     | Backpack    |
| Umbrella     | Crown            | Flashlight | Skateboard  |
| Rope         | Magnifying Glass | Guitar     | Chess Piece |

---

# Story Requirements

Your animation must include:

* A main character
* A goal, challenge, or problem
* A meaningful use of the required object
* A clear ending

The audience should be able to understand the basic story without explanation.

---

# Animation Requirements

Your project must include at least **four** of the following techniques:

* Walk Cycle
* Anticipation and Overshoot
* Smear Frames
* Impact Flash
* Environmental Effects
* Lighting Effects
* Camera Movement
* Parallax
* Tweening

Students are encouraged to include more than four techniques when appropriate.

---

# Production Rule

Students who fall behind a checkpoint may be required to reduce project scope.

Professional productions frequently adjust scope to meet deadlines.

**A finished 30-second animation is worth more than an unfinished 90-second masterpiece.**

---

# Production Pipeline

Professional productions are completed in stages.

Students must complete each stage before moving on.

---

# CHECKPOINT 1 – Concept Development

## Due: End of Day 1

### Story Summary

Write a short summary describing:

* Main character
* Goal or challenge
* Required object
* Ending

### Initial Concept Sketches

Create rough sketches showing:

* Character ideas
* Environment ideas
* Important props

---

## Submission Requirements

* Story Summary
* Character Concepts
* Environment Concepts

---

# CHECKPOINT 2 – Storyboard

## Due: End of Day 2

Create a storyboard showing the major events of the animation.

### Requirements

Minimum:

* 6 storyboard panels

Recommended:

* 8–12 storyboard panels

Each panel should show:

* Major action
* Camera position
* Important transitions

Dialogue is optional.

---

## Submission Requirements

* Completed storyboard

---

# CHECKPOINT 3 – Rough Animation Pass

## Due: End of Day 4

Create a rough animation showing:

* Timing
* Camera movement
* Major actions
* Scene transitions

The animation does not need color or details.

Focus on motion and storytelling.

### Goals

* Establish timing
* Test camera movement
* Confirm story clarity
* Identify missing assets before production continues

---

## Submission Requirements

* Rough Animation Export
* Working Project File

---

# CHECKPOINT 4 – Base Color Pass

## Due: End of Day 6

Apply flat colors to:

* Characters
* Props
* Environments

Focus on consistency and readability.

Do not begin detailed rendering.

### Goals

* Complete all major artwork
* Establish color palette
* Improve visual readability
* Ensure all scenes are complete

---

## Submission Requirements

* Updated Animation Export
* Working Project File

---

# CHECKPOINT 5 – Shading and Lighting Pass

## Due: End of Day 8

Add:

* Shadows
* Highlights
* Glow Effects
* Atmospheric Lighting

Focus attention on important story moments.

### Goals

* Increase visual depth
* Improve mood and atmosphere
* Direct audience attention
* Enhance storytelling through lighting

---

## Submission Requirements

* Updated Animation Export
* Working Project File

---

# CHECKPOINT 6 – Final Polish Pass

## Due: End of Day 10

Complete:

* Cleanup
* Timing adjustments
* Visual effects
* Layer organization
* Export testing

Students should avoid major story or animation changes during this stage.

### Goals

* Refine existing work
* Improve presentation quality
* Fix technical issues
* Prepare final export

---

## Submission Requirements

* Near-Final Animation
* Final Project File

# Success Strategy

Students who succeed on this project:

1. Complete checkpoints on time.
2. Keep their story simple.
3. Reuse assets whenever possible.
4. Focus on clear storytelling.
5. Finish rough animation early.
6. Save polish for the final days.

---

# Key Lesson

Professional animators do not create great work by working endlessly.

They create great work by following a plan, managing their time effectively, and making smart production decisions.
